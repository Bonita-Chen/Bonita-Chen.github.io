One of the most useful lessons from my Bio-Techne internship was that a dashboard becomes a very different product once it has to scale.

At first, the reporting need was relatively contained. Over time, the scope widened: more entities, more invoices, more cross-region context, and more people depending on the same source of truth.

## What scaling changed

### Definitions had to become explicit

When only one team uses a dashboard, a lot can stay implicit. Once the audience grows, every KPI definition needs to hold up under new users, new questions, and new regions.

### Data modeling mattered as much as visual design

The most visible part of a dashboard is the front end, but the real leverage came from structuring the data well enough that the views stayed reliable.

### Self-serve reporting saves time twice

It saves time once because people can find their own answer. It saves time again because the same recurring question stops interrupting the team.

That project made me appreciate dashboards as communication tools, not just reporting artifacts.

My first surprise about Amazon analytics was how much of the work sits between language and data.

At Goat Consulting, keyword research was never just about finding high-volume phrases. It was really about learning how customers describe a product, what tradeoffs they care about first, and how those choices should shape the structure of a listing. Good performance reporting helped, but so did careful wording.

## Three lessons that stayed with me

### 1. Search visibility starts with interpretation

A keyword list only becomes useful when it turns into better copy. I learned to think about titles, bullets, and A+ Content as a translation layer between what a customer types and what a brand wants to say.

### 2. Reporting matters most when it changes behavior

Pulling weekly metrics is easy. Turning those metrics into a specific recommendation is harder. The most valuable part of the work was summarizing what changed, why it might have changed, and what we should test next.

### 3. The operational details are part of the strategy

Maintaining clean internal tracking, keeping campaign notes current, and staying consistent across accounts may not sound glamorous, but they make better analysis possible later.

## What I’m taking forward

This internship made me appreciate how closely content, experimentation, and measurement depend on each other. It also reminded me that analytics is often at its best when it helps a team decide the next small move with more confidence.

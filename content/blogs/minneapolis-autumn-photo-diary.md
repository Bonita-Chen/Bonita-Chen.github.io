There are seasons that make a city feel newly legible. Autumn in Minneapolis does that for me.

The light softens, the paths around campus slow down slightly, and familiar places start to look more deliberate. This is one of the reasons I keep photographing the same spaces: a place changes long before we think it does.

## A few things I notice every fall

- Campus looks warmer and more cinematic in late afternoon than I remember from summer.
- The city feels quieter without becoming empty.
- Photography helps me pay attention to details I would otherwise hurry past.

I like having at least one part of the site that is less about productivity and more about noticing.
